Charitable Donations

Covid-19 has left a lot of people unemployed, helpless, and in immediate need of food or funds. Countless charities, organizations, and non-profits have stepped up during these hard times to help those in need. Despite their efforts, the devastating financial impact of Covid-19 will continue to be felt long after the virus is over.

Have the websites confused you? Are some things to complicated to understand? We understand that! Click here for a simple list of charities that are in need of donations or volunteers.

Below is information on different groups trying to make a difference. Please take some time and see how you can help out, or get help if needed:

Heart to Heart International

  • Purpose: Helping in the medical field
  • Location: Across the US
  • How To Help: Spread Awareness on their mission

"Across the U.S., HHI is distributing hygiene kits for those in quarantine or unable to leave their homes. While personal protection supplies are limited globally, HHI has collected and distributed items, including masks, gowns, and sanitizers. HHI remains an active member of healthcare coalitions, providing gap support so that service organizations can continue to operate."- HHI Website



Sewa International

  • Purpose: Setting up non-medical helplines
  • Location: Houston, Bay Area, NJ, Chicago, Denver, Atlanta
  • How To Help: Volunteer to help manage helplines

"Sewa International has been active on the ground early, to help deal with the Coronavirus pandemic situation. We have established a Non-Medical helplines in different areas of USA, which anyone can call"- Sewa International, Houston


Save The Children

  • Purpose: Supporting children who need school for safety, learning, food
  • Location: Conflict Zones, Refugee Camps, and Overcrowded Communities
  • How To Help: Donations supplies and funds

"In the United States alone, 30 million children who rely on school for both learning and meals are at great risk. Save the Children is also distributing hygiene supplies to ensure communities can wash their hands, and vital health information on how to stay safe and prevent the spread of infection." - STC Website



United Way Worldwide

  • Purpose: Launched a national response and recovery fund
  • Location: Nationwide
  • How To Help: Spread Awareness and Donate Funds

"Till now, United Way has helped millions of individuals find the help they need, often by answering questions related to health care, financial assistance and much more, through 211 (their direct hotline). UW has also worked with corporate partners to donate needed funds and goods and services to those in need. And advocated hard for the U.S. government to provide adequate stimulus funding so charities doing life-saving work could keep their doors open as resources dwindle." -UWW



Big Table

  • Purpose: Saving restaurants across the US
  • Location: Seattle, San Diego, Spokane
  • How To Help: Volunteer and Refer Businesses that need help

"Big Table is offering their support in helping care for those in crisis in the restaurant and hospital industry. They are doing so by setting up Take Out Services, Gift Cards, Safety Networks, and Community Awareness." - Big Table Website
